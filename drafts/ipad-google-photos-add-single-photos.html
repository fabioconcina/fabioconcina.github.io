Add single photos from iPad to Google Photos

April 22, 2019

iCloud services and Google services are hard to mix. I mainly use Google services for my routine, but I sometimes also make use of some iOS and Mac devices.
The problem I was facing was to manually add a picture from my iPad to Google Photos. The issue raised only on the iOS device, because:

  • from Mac/PC I can add a picture using the web application at photos.google.com
  • from Android I have set an automatic sync of all my gallery, plus there is the option to add a single photo using the share menu

Funny enough, I had an hard time loading a photo from my iPad. I often process my DSLR pictures directly and the iPad, and I really liked the possibility to then load them straight to Google Photos.
There is the option to sync all the gallery automatically (which I didn't want to do, as I may have many other images I don't want to upload), but I couldn't find any option from the share menu both in iOS Photos and iOS Files to upload a photo to Google Photos.

This is the workaround I found that lets me add single photos to Google Photos from the iPad:

  • from Safari, open the web application at photos.google.com, instead of using the iOS app
  • using iOS multitasking, open in split screen iOS Photos app
  • simply drag the picture from iOS Photos to Safari, and the web interface from Google Photos will upload the picture as in the desktop version.

Hope that this could be useful to someone else, and if you know an alternative solution let me know in the comments below.

Note: tested on iOS 12.x
